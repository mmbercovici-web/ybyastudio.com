Casa Lupa

Set on the coast of Rio Grande do Norte, Casa Lupa is conceived as a compact dwelling that works with breeze, shade and the slow heat of the day. A rammed‑earth envelope and thickened walls provide thermal inertia, while a sequence of shaded rooms is organised to secure cross‑ventilation and reduce mechanical cooling. Openings are calibrated to filter light and frame vegetation and sky, letting climate and time draw subtle variations across mineral surfaces.
