Wood collective

Set within São Paulo’s dense fabric, Wood collective proposes a calm domestic field where shared circulation and private rooms remain loosely connected. A timber structure organizes the plan with repeating bays, allowing light, ventilation and program to adapt over time. Shaded thresholds and filtered openings frame fragments of city and vegetation, letting climate and daily use register quietly in the material. Public areas remain generous yet understated, with circulation conceived as a social spine that encourages incidental encounters while preserving quiet retreat.
