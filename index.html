Position

Material-first architecture is not nostalgia. It is a technical stance where comfort is embedded in mass, orientation, shade depth, and air paths — not added later by systems.

  • Earth, timber, stone — honest junctions.
  • Cross-ventilation as plan logic.
  • Solar protection and rain intelligence.
  • Courtyards and verandas as climate devices.
  • Lean process, high presence on site.

Manifest

Architecture as inhabitable landscape

Projects begin with the site’s physics: sun, wind, water, ground. Form follows comfort.

We work where climate is a design tool and matter is a method. Instead of adding performance later, we draw it into the section: shadow, mass, and air as primary structure.

The goal is not spectacle. It is a quiet precision: spaces that age well, accept weather, and keep the daily rhythm intact.

  • Thermal calm through mass and delayed heat transfer.
  • Openings sized for air paths, not façade composition.
  • Deep shade as structure: verandas, porches, galleries.
  • Water and topography treated as a project system.
  • Detailing as ethics: fewer materials, clearer assemblies.
Studio

Between site, model, and landscape

From early studies to site direction, with lean processes and high presence on site.

YBYÁ STUDIO works across architecture, interiors, and hospitality — with a material-first approach grounded in tropical and subtropical climates.

We collaborate closely with local knowledge, suppliers, and builders, shaping projects through prototypes, mockups, and precise detailing.

Working method
Reduction: fewer devices, clearer assemblies, stronger climates. Comfort is designed as a property of form and material — not as an afterthought.
